Principles

  • My two favourite things to do are: Creating and Learning. They also both naturally complement each other i.e. the more you create, the more you learn. The more you learn, the more you CAN create.
  • Lifelong and continual learning. Learning is the spice of life. Learning is how your brain grows. Learning can be one of the greatest providers of happiness. Meta-Learning i.e. Learning how to learn. Once you learn how to learn well, you can learn anything. Therefore learning anything helps your ability to learn other unrelated things. It is sad that the state of education today often discourages learning more than it encourages it.
  • Contrary to what most people think instinctively, you CAN get smarter. The mind is malleable and it is technically correct to say that learning causes permanent changes to your brain (it get's heavier) and that it is similar in a sense to muscle (it gets stronger through use). Anyone can learn anything, there are just people who think they can or can't. And slightly more controversial: talent doesn't exist. Or at least it should be MADE to not exist as it is one of the most harmful words in the dictionary. The reasons I believe this are:
    1. People who are told they are talented then don't try as hard because they are called "talented" and they subconsciously believe life gave them a great hand.
    2. while the "non-talented" people look on sadly in disdain at themselves because they believe life has dealt them an unfortunate hand.
    3. People often give up before really even trying because they aren't "talented". It took me 6 years of Classical Guitar to finally start enjoying it.
    4. Also just Believing You Can Get Smarter Makes You Smarter... "As compared to the control group, students who learned about intelligence's malleability had higher academic motivation, better academic behavior, and better grades in mathematics."
    5. Fixed mindset vs Growth mindset. Believing in growth mindset/self-improvement is just a ton of bricks more exciting than Fixed mindset.
      “Talent is a pursued interest. Anything that you're willing to practice, you can do.” - Bob Ross
    No good can come from this word. For people that still don't agree, at least this should be true for you:
    “Hard work beats talent when talent doesn't work hard.” ― Tim Notke
    For many years of my life I played online games (MMORPGs) excessively. I then realised that I'd rather make my own life a Role Playing Game (with me as the main character) as this is an infinitely more productive and better use of my time.
  • I like to make everything as simple as possible by using logic, breaking it into sub-parts, reorganising, focusing and using a first-principles approach. This has proven to be very effective again and again for all parts of my life, from music to programming to reading to writing to understanding very complicated ideas etc.
  • Focus is key. Great good can be accomplished through focus. It's not only how hard you work, it's also how smart and focused you work. One man's hour can accomplish what takes another man ten. Also on a related note, the 10,000 hour rule can be very discouraging to many people, you can get subjectively excellent at things within a 100 hours.
  • Two factors to personal success: Belief and Hard Work (which is Smart and Focused aswell). Self-help books complicate this by making this list 100 points long.
  • Two factors to business success: A good idea and good execution of said idea. Everyone and their grandmother has 20 great ideas, good execution is much harder to find.
  • I'm not a "glass half full" kinda guy, I'm a "Oh my god, there's lots left, take it slowly and savour it" kinda guy.
  • You have to do the difficult things in life to make life not difficult. The more difficult things you do, things of even greater difficulty become easier and within reach of your abilities. And things of similar difficulty obviously also become easier. But most of all, things of low difficulty become a "walk in the park"/"do it in your sleep" easy.
  • Summarising programming: if (anything): doAnything()
